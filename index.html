The Science

What is thermoeconomics?

Thermoeconomics sits at the intersection of physics, information theory, computation, and economics. It treats economic value not as an abstraction, but as something grounded in physical reality ‚Äî energy expended, entropy produced, work performed.

The field draws on Shannon's information theory (1948), Jaynes' maximum entropy principle (1957), Wolfram's computational universe, and the living mathematics of blockchain proof-of-work systems.

This isn't speculative philosophy. It's a framework you can compute ‚Äî and this course teaches you how, step by step, in both Wolfram Language and Python.

Blockchain Architectures

The UTXO model and its many flavors

From Bitcoin's original design to the exotic variants powering modern chains ‚Äî the UTXO Alliance maps the entire landscape.

High-level blockchain architecture ‚Äî how transactions flow from creation to consensus
The building blocks ‚Äî transactions, blocks, and the chain that connects them

Bitcoin introduced the Unspent Transaction Output (UTXO) model ‚Äî a fundamentally different way of tracking value on a blockchain. Instead of accounts with balances, the system tracks individual outputs waiting to be spent.

Since Bitcoin, dozens of chains have taken this foundation and built on it ‚Äî adding smart contracts, sharding, merged mining, and more. The UTXO Alliance brings these projects together to advance the model. The knowledge base includes a comprehensive visual guide to every major UTXO variant, adapted from the Alliance's official handbook.
